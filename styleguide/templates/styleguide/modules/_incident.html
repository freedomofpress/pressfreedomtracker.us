Arrest

Voactiv journalist charged with rioting in Washington

January 20, 2017

Police arrested Evan Engel, a senior producer at the news website Vocativ, near 12th and L streets in downtown Washington the morning of January 20. Engel is charged with rioting that results in bodily harm or more than $5,000 in damage, which can be punished by a maximum of 10 years in jail and fines of up to $25,000, according to The U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of Columbia.

The reporters were among more than 230 people arrested in Washington on Inauguration Day after individuals set fire to a car and broke windows of downtown businesses.

Police also seized Engel's camera and mobile phone.

January 27, 2017

The U.S. Attorney’s office announced on Jan. 27 that it was dropping charges against Engel, saying, “After consultation with the counsel for Mr. Engel, who is a journalist with Vocativ, as well as a review of evidence presented to us by law enforcement, we have concluded that we will not proceed with the charge against this individual.”
